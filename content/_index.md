# We stand on the shoulders of giants.

This project documents the history of advocacy for **vulnerability disclosure**, **coordinated disclosure**, and — more broadly — **the rights of those who hack in good faith**.

The focus is on **people and their stories**, particularly those who worked hard behind the scenes to make things happen. We trace the **"who drafted from whom"** lineage: which policies borrowed language from which; which individuals mentored, cited, or handed the baton to whom; which quiet staffers and legal drafters wrote the words that later shaped an industry.

> Some of the giants are loud. Others are quiet. Both deserve to be remembered.

## What lives here

- **Timelines** — chronologies compiled from primary sources, each archived for permanence.
- **People** — profiles of contributors, both public-facing and behind-the-scenes, with the threads that connect them.
- **Documents** — drafts, memos, and policy text with drafting provenance where known.
- **Lineage** — the influence graph: who drafted from whom, and how ideas traveled.

## Principles

1. **Attribution accuracy comes before narrative.** When uncertain, say so. Misattribution compounds across generations.
2. **Surface the quiet work.** The person who drafted the language matters as much as the person who announced it.
3. **Permanence by default.** Every source link is archived (archive.ph and/or the Wayback Machine) so the trail doesn't rot.
4. **Primary sources over retellings.** Where possible, link the original memo, not a summary of it.
