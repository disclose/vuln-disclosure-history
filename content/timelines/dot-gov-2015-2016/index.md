## Background

> Over the past two years, as a part of growing concerns around cyber security and how it affects many aspects of US citizen and consumer life, the federal government has put forth a wide variety of recommendations to improve cyber security posture, of which coordinated disclosure is a significant and recurring component.

*Infographic updated December 2016, produced by [I Am The Cavalry](https://www.iamthecavalry.org) ([archive.ph](https://archive.ph/newest/https://www.iamthecavalry.org)).*

![Dot Gov Coordinated Disclosure Timeline](dot-gov-coordinated-disclosure-timeline-2016.png)

## 2015

- **05/2015 — House Energy & Commerce (HEC)** committee sends out a formal written request to auto manufacturers raising questions about concerns with cyber security, including relationships with the research community and promotion of coordinated disclosure.
- **06/2015 — Federal Trade Commission (FTC)** announces the "Start with Security" (SWS) initiative, with a publication for best practices and series of future workshops. Disclosure is covered in the recommendations document and is a key topic in the workshops later in the year.
- **09/2015 — Federal Trade Commission (FTC)** hosts a Start With Security conference in San Francisco. **The Department of Commerce's National Telecommunications & Information Administration (NTIA)** conducts the first of a series of workshops on identifying the state of the art in Coordinated Disclosure practices.
- **10/2015 — Library of Congress (LoC)** Librarian enacts the most recent round of exemptions to the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) that allow for exemptions for cyber security research into automotive and medical systems, as well as jailbreaking phones and tablets.
- **11/2015 — FTC** hosts the second SWS conference in Austin. Vulnerability reporting and disclosure is again a prominently featured topic on the agenda.
- **12/2015 — NTIA** hosts its second workshop on Coordinated Disclosure best practices in Washington DC.

## 2016

- **01/2016 — Food & Drug Administration (FDA)** hosts a workshop outside of Washington DC to discuss cybersecurity best practices for medical device manufacturers, and then issues a draft guidance document for security recommendations, which includes the need for supporting coordinated disclosure on the part of said device manufacturers.
- **02/2016 — House Energy & Commerce** discussion on Coordinated Disclosure.
- **03/2016 — Department of Defense** announces the "Hack the Pentagon" initiative, a time-bounded bug bounty program executed in conjunction with HackerOne, which centers around coordinated disclosure of vulnerabilities in public-facing, non-mission-critical DoD websites.
- **04/2016 — NTIA** hosts its third workshop on Coordinated Disclosure best practices in Chicago.
- **10/2016**
  - **Hack the Pentagon 2.0** was awarded.
  - **NHTSA** — U.S. DOT issues guidance to the automotive industry.
  - **DMCA** security research exemption for consumer devices begin coordinated disclosure meeting in Washington D.C.
  - **The Library of Congress** announces temporary exemptions from the DMCA for researchers working with consumer, medical, and automotive devices take effect.
- **11/2016**
  - **Department of Homeland Security** issues guidance for Safety Critical IoT.
  - **Department of Defense** publishes first DoD Coordinated Disclosure Policy and launches Hack The Army bug bounty program.
  - **The General Services Administration's Technology Transfer Service** released The Vulnerability Disclosure Policy granting researchers a legal way to detect and notify the agency of security vulnerabilities on a number of public-facing government websites.

## People & Drafting Lineage

*This page is a first-pass transcription of institutional events. The [mission of this repo]({{< ref "/" >}}) is to surface the **people** — both loud and quiet — who did the drafting, shepherded the process, and passed the baton. Each event above will be enriched with named contributors and their drafting/influence lineage as research completes. Contributions welcome via [issue or PR](https://github.com/disclose/vuln-disclosure-history).*

**Known entities whose individual contributors are candidates for enrichment:**

- **I Am The Cavalry** (infographic author) — grassroots coalition; specific volunteers and co-founders to be attributed with citations.
- **NTIA multistakeholder process** — the convener(s) and the working-group drafters who produced the output documents.
- **Hack the Pentagon / DoD VDP** — Defense Digital Service staff who championed the program and drafted the first DoD-wide VDP.
- **18F / GSA VDP** — the small team that wrote the first U.S. federal civilian agency VDP.
- **House Energy & Commerce** — committee staff who drafted the 2015 letter to auto manufacturers.
- **FTC "Start with Security"** — Bureau of Consumer Protection staff who shaped the recommendations and agenda.
- **Library of Congress DMCA exemption (§1201)** — researchers, counsel, and EFF/advocacy drafters who built the record that secured the security-research exemption.
- **FDA medical device cyber guidance** — CDRH staff who drafted the pre-market / post-market guidance language on coordinated disclosure.
- **NHTSA automotive cybersecurity guidance** — Office of Defects Investigation and policy staff who drafted the industry guidance.
- **DHS Safety Critical IoT principles** — the drafters of the strategic principles document.
